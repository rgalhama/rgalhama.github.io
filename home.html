Hello, world!

I am an Assistant Professor on Artificial Intelligence for text and human interaction, at the Institute of Logic, Language and Computation, University of Amsterdam. My research focuses on computational modelling of language learning, on topics that range from language acquisition, sentence processing and language emergence, among others.

Before, I was an Assistant Professor at the Cognitive Science and Artificial Intelligence department, Tilburg University.

Earlier, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics. I worked with Evan Kidd and Caroline Rowland on computational modelling of word learning. Previously, I worked with Ram Frost at the Basque Center on Cognition, Brain and Language, modeling visual word recognition with a combination of information-theoretic metrics and neural approaches.

I did my PhD at the Institute of Logic, Language and Computation, at the University of Amsterdam. My thesis, supervised by Jelle Zuidema, Remko Scha and Carel Ten Cate, focused on Computational Modelling of Artificial Language Learning.
