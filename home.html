Hello, world!

I am an Assistant Professor at the Cognitive Science and Artificial Intelligence department, at Tilburg University. My research focuses on computational modelling of language learning, on topics that range from auditory and visual statistical learning to structure learning, sentence processing and language development.

Before, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics. I worked with Evan Kidd and Caroline Rowland on computational modelling of word learning. Previously, I worked at the Basque Center on Cognition, Brain and Language, investigating the effects of entropy on visual word recognition with Blair Armstrong and Ram Frost.

I did my PhD at the Institute of Logic, Language and Computation, at the University of Amsterdam. My thesis, supervised by Jelle Zuidema, Remko Scha and Carel Ten Cate, focused on Computational Modelling of Artificial Language Learning.
